WHO IS THE PRESIDENT?

The President of the Grand Assembly serves as the presiding officer of the legislature and is responsible for maintaining order, managing legislative proceedings, and certifying the passage of laws.

The President ensures the proper functioning of legislative sessions in accordance with the Constitution and the Standing Orders of the Grand Assembly.

DUTIES AND AUTHORITIES

The duties of the President include:

  • Presiding over Assembly sessions
  • Maintaining order and enforcing codes of conduct
  • Managing the legislative agenda
  • Assigning bill designations
  • Certifying legislative passage
  • Representing the Grand Assembly in official capacity
  • Maintaining official legislative records in absence of the Clerk
