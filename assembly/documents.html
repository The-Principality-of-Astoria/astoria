Standing Orders of the Grand Assembly

For Crown and People

Issued under the authority of the Constitution of the Principality of Astoria.

ARTICLE I - PURPOSE AND AUTHORITY

Section 1: Purpose

These Standing Orders establish the procedural rules governing the operation of the Grand Assembly of Astoria and define the method by which legislation is introduced, debated, voted upon, certified, and recorded.

Section 2: Constitutional Authority

These Standing Orders operate under the Constitution of the Principality of Astoria and the sovereign authority of the Princess Regnant.

Nothing herein shall limit or restrict the sovereign authority of the Crown.

ARTICLE II - SESSIONS OF THE GRAND ASSEMBLY

Section 1: Regular Sessions

The Grand Assembly shall convene in regular sessions twice per month, on the second and fourth Wednesdays of each month.

Additional sessions may be convened if summoned by:

  • The Princess Regnant, or
  • The President of the Grand Assembly

For any reason they deem fit.

Section 2: Monthly Schedule

At the beginning of each month, the Acting President shall publish a monthly schedule establishing the dates of the Assembly's regular sessions. This schedule shall be accessible to the public.

Prior to each session, the Acting President shall issue a Schedule of the Day to all Members of the Grand Assembly.

This schedule shall include:

  • All bills to be considered
  • Any matters to be debated
  • Any additional legislative business

Section 3: Attendance Requirement

Members of the Grand Assembly are required to attend all scheduled sessions unless a justifiable reason is submitted to the Acting President.

Two unexcused absences shall subject the Member to disciplinary review as deemed fit by the Acting President.

Section 4: Absence of Presiding Officers

If both the President and Deputy President are absent, the session shall be postponed.

If urgent legislative matters require immediate action and postponement is not possible, the senior-most Member of the Grand Assembly shall preside.

ARTICLE III - QUORUM

Quorum shall consist of twelve Members of the Grand Assembly.

No legislative business may be conducted without quorum.

ARTICLE IV - OFFICERS OF THE GRAND ASSEMBLY

Section 1: Acting President

The Acting President shall be:

  • The President, or
  • The Deputy President, when acting in the President's absence

Section 2: Authority of the Acting President

The Acting President shall,

  • Preside over sessions
  • Maintain order
  • Recognize Members to speak
  • Establish session agendas
  • Assign bill designations
  • Certify legislative results
  • Enforce conduct rules

Section 3: Clerk of the Grand Assembly

The Clerk, when appointed, shall:

  • Maintain legislative records
  • Archive legislation
  • Assign bill designation when authorized

Until a Clerk is appointed, these duties shall be performed by the Acting President

ARTICLE V - INTRODUCTION OF LEGISLATION

Section 1: Submission of Bills

Bills may be submitted:

  • Prior to a session, or
  • At the beginning of a session

Prior submission is recommended.

Section 2: Bill Designation

Bills shall be assigned a legislative number in the format:

AST-L-YEAR-NUMBER

Bill numbers shall be assigned by:

  • The President, or
  • The Clerk when authorized

Section 3: Urgent Legislation

Bills deemed urgent by the Acting President may be placed immediately on the debate floor.

Such bills shall still undergo full debate and voting procedure.

ARTICLE VI - SESSION PROCEDURE

Section 1: Opening of Session

At the beginning of each session, the Acting President shall request any urgent matters requiring immediate attention be brought to the floor.

Such matters shall be addressed before scheduled legislative business.

Section 2: Reading of Bills

Each bill shall be read in full or summarized.

Members shall be given opportunity to ask questions.

Section 3: Debate

Debates shall follow queue system regulated by the Acting President.

Members may speak when recognized, and the Acting President may end debate when appropriate.

ARTICLE VII - AMENDMENTS

Amendments may be proposed verbally or in writing.

Amendments shall require a simple majority vote of Members present.

Approved amendments must be entered into the official Assembly record.

ARTICLE VIII - VOTING

Section 1: Calling the Vote

Following debate and amendment, the Acting president shall call for a vote.

Section 2: Voting Method

Members shall vote only one of the following:

  • Aye
  • Nay
  • Abstain

Majority shall be calculated based on votes cast.

The Acting president shall announce the results of the vote once all votes are cast.

Section 3: Tie Votes

In the event of a tie, the Acting President shall cast the deciding vote.

Section 4: Required Majorities

All constitutional voting thresholds shall apply.

ARTICLE IX - CERTIFICATION AND CROWN ACTION

Upon passage, the Acting President shall certify the legislation.

The legislation shall be transmitted to the Princess Regnant for sovereign approval or denial.

Upon approval by the Princess Regnant, the legislation shall become law immediately unless otherwise specified.

ARTICLE X - RECORDKEEPING

All passed legislation shall be archived.

Records shall include:

  • Bill designation
  • Title
  • Date passed
  • Date approved
  • Full text

ARTICLE XI - CONDUCT AND DISCIPLINE

Section 1: Conduct

Members shall not disgrace:

  • Themselves
  • The Assembly
  • The Principality
  • The Citizens
  • The Crown

Disruption of official Assembly procedure is prohibited

Section 2: Disciplinary Authority

The Acting President may:

  • End a Member's speaking privileges
  • Remove a bill from agenda
  • Remove a Member from the session
  • Suspend a Member's duties pending further action

ARTICLE XII - EFFECTIVE DATE

These Standing Orders shall take effect immediately upon adoption
