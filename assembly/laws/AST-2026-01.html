ARTICLE I - ESTABLISHMENT OF THE NATIONAL CURRENCY

Section 1: Creation of the Currency

The official currency of the Principality of Astoria is hereby established as the Astorian Crown.

The Astorian Crown shall serve as the official unit of account of the government of Astoria.

Section 2: Currency Symbols and Denominations

The Astorian Crown shall be designated by:

  • Symbol: AC
  • International Code: ASC

The Astorian Crown shall be divided into one hundred subunits known as Marks, designated by the symbol "Mk".

Section 3: Legal Status

The Astorian Crown shall be recognized as the sole official accounting currency of the Government of the Principality of Astoria for all public financial purposes.

The currency may exist in ledger, accounting, or other official recorded form and shall not require physical issuance to be valid.

ARTICLE II - AUTHORITY OVER THE CURRENCY

Section 1: Issuance Authority

The issuance of the Astorian Crown shall occur only through:

  • Appropriations authorized by law through the national budget, or
  • Royal Decree issued by the Princess Regnant

Section 2: Sovereign Authority

Nothing in this Act shall limit or restrict the sovereign monetary authority of the Princess Regnant as established under the Constitution

ARTICLE III - ESTABLISHMENT OF THE TREASURY

Section 1: Treasury Authority

The Ministry of the Treasury shall be the official administrative authority responsible for managing the currency and financial operations of the Government of Astoria.

The Minister of the Treasury shall oversee the daily operations of the Treasury and may delegate tasks as necessary.

Section 2: Treasury Responsibilities

The Treasury shall:

  • tain financial accounts of the government
  • Administer funds appropriated by law
  • Manage government reserves
  • Record all issuance of the Astorian Crown

Section 3: Treasury Ledger

The Treasury shall maintain an official financial ledger.

The ledger shall include:

  • Total currency issued
  • Government balances
  • Budget allocations
  • Government expenditures
  • Reserve holdings

The Treasury ledger shall be public.

Section 4: Financial Reports

The Treasury shall publish an official financial report at least once per fiscal year.

Additional reports may be issued when ordered by law or by Royal Decree.

ARTICLE IV - GOVERNMENT BUDGET

Section 1: Fiscal Year

The fiscal year of the Principality of Astoria shall begin on the 1st of January and conclude on the 31st of December.

Section 2: Budget Preparation and Submission

The Minister of the Treasury may prepare a proposed national budget.

The proposed budget shall be submitted to the Grand Assembly by the President of the Grand Assembly no later than one week prior to the beginning of the fiscal year.

Section 3: Budget Approval

The national budget shall require approval by the Grand Assembly in accordance with constitutional voting requirements.

Upon approval the budget shall be certified by the President of the Grand Assembly.

Section 4: Temporary Budget Authority

If the Grand Assembly fails to approve a national budget before the beginning of the fiscal year, a temporary budget shall automatically take effect.

The temporary budget shall be based on the appropriations and spending levels of the previous fiscal year.

The temporary budget shall remain in force until a new budget is approved.

ARTICLE V - EXPENDITURE AUTHORITY

Section 1: Treasury Control of Funds

The Minister of the Treasury shall authorize government expenditures consistent with the approved budget or lawful authority.

Section 2: Ministry Spending Authority

Government ministries may expend funds allocated to them in the national budget.

Funds specifically appropriated for designated purposes must be used for those purposes.

Remaining allocated funds may be used at the discretion of the ministry unless otherwise restricted by law.

Section 3: Sovereign Authority under the Crown

The Princess Regnant retains sovereign authority to authorize expenditures consistent with her constitutional powers.

ARTICLE VI - SEPARATION OF THE ROYAL ESTATE

Section 1: Royal Estate Independence

The Royal Estate shall remain separate from the Government Treasury.

The Royal Estate shall be administered solely under the authority of the Princess Regnant.

Section 2: Treasury Independence

The Government Treasury and Royal Estate shall remain distinct financial entities.

ARTICLE VII - OVERSIGHT AND ACCOUNTABILITY

Section 1: Legislative Oversight

The Grand Assembly shall have authority to review Treasury operations and financial records.

Section 2: Public Record

Treasury records and approved budgets shall be posted in a public manner.

ARTICLE VIII - EFFECTIVE DATE

This Act shall take effect immediately upon its lawful passage and publication.
