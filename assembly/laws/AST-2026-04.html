ARTICLE I - ESTABLISHMENT OF THE CIVIL SERVICE

Section 1: Establishment

There shall exist a Civil Service of the Principality of Astoria.

Section 2: Definition

Civil Servants shall consist of non-political government employees serving within the Ministries and institutions of the Principality.

Civil Servants shall not include:

  • The Princess Regnant
  • The Chief Minister
  • Ministers
  • Members of the Grand Assembly
  • Judiciary officers

Section 3: Scope

Judiciary administrative staff shall be considered Civil Servants.

Law enforcement officers and related personnel shall be governed by this Act except where superseded by a subsequent act.

ARTICLE II - ADMINISTRATION AND AUTHORITY

Section 1: Ministerial Administration

The Civil Service shall be administered separately within each Ministry.

Section 2: Hiring Authority

Civil Servants shall be appointed under delegated authority from the Princess Regnant through the Minister of the respective Ministry.

Section 3: Sovereign Authority

Nothing in this Act shall limit the authority of the Princess Regnant to appoint, remove, or override decisions regarding any Civil Servant.

ARTICLE III - ELIGIBILITY

Section 1: Minimum Age

Persons must be at least sixteen years of age to serve as a Civil Servant.

Section 2: Citizenship Requirement

Civil Servants must be citizens of the Principality of Astoria.

Section 3: Exceptions

The respective Ministry may designate certain non-sensitive positions as exempt from citizenship requirements.

Section 4: Background Checks

All Civil Servants shall undergo appropriate background checks prior to appointment.

Section 5: Exception - Ministry of Defense

The Ministry of Defense may impose stricter eligibility requirements for its personnel.

ARTICLE IV - APPOINTMENT AND PROBATION

Section 1: Appointment

Appointments shall be made in accordance with regulations established by the respective Ministry.

Section 2: Probationary Period

Standard Civil Servants shall serve a probationary period of six months.

Employees of the Ministry of Defense shall serve a probationary period of one year.

ARTICLE V - DUTIES AND CONDUCT

Section 1: Political Neutrality

Civil Servants shall keep partisan political activities separate from their official duties.

Section 2: Public Conduct

Civil Servants may express personal opinions publicly, provided they clearly state such opinions as their own and not those of the Government.

Civil Servants shall not act in a manner that disgraces the image of the Government or its employees.

ARTICLE VI - COMPENSATION

Section 1: Salary Determination

Compensation of Civil Servants shall be determined by the respective Ministries.

Salaries of Civil Servants shall be public information.

Section 2: Budgetary Limitations

All compensation and expenditure shall remain within the limits established by the national budget.

ARTICLE VII - DISCIPLINE AND REMOVAL

Section 1: Removal For Cause

Civil Servants may be removed for cause in accordance with Ministerial regulations.

Section 2: Ministerial Authority

Discipline and removal shall be governed by regulations established by the respective Ministries.

Section 3: Appeal

A Civil Servant may appeal disciplinary action or removal through the supervisory chain of command, culminating with the Chief Minister.

The Princess Regnant retains the authority to review or override any civil service disciplinary or appeal decision.

ARTICLE VIII - PROTECTIONS

Section 1: Protection from Arbitrary Dismissal

Civil Servants shall not be removed without cause except by authority of the Princess Regnant.

Section 2: Whistleblower Protection

Civil Servants shall not face retaliation for reporting unlawful conduct.

Section 3: Refusal of Unlawful Orders

Civil Servants may refuse to carry out unlawful orders without penalties.

Section 4: Lawful Immunity

Civil Servants acting lawfully within the scope of their duties shall be protected from personal liability.

ARTICLE IX - REGULATORY AUTHORITY

Section 1: Ministerial Regulations

Each Ministry may establish regulations governing hiring, discipline, internal conduct, and operational standards.

Section 2: Delegation

Ministers may delegate administrative authority to subordinate officials within their Ministry.

ARTICLE X - EFFECTIVE DATE

This act shall take effect immediately upon its lawful passage and publication.
