ARTICLE I - GENERAL PRINCIPLES

Section 1: Authority of Law

This Code shall define criminal offenses within the Principality of Astoria.

All persons within the jurisdiction of the Principality shall be subject to this Code.

Section 2: Presumption of Innocence

All accused persons shall be presumed innocent until proven guilty.

Section 3: Standard of Liability

A person may be found guilty where:

  • The act was committed intentionally
  • The act was committed through gross negligence sufficient to cause harm or violate law

Gross negligence shall consist of a reckless disregard for substantial and foreseeable harm.

Section 4: Jurisdiction

This Code shall apply to:

  • Citizens of Astoria
  • Non-citizens within Astorian jurisdiction
  • Civil Servants and Government officials
  • Members of the Armed Forces, subject to additional but separate military law
  • Citizens of Astoria for acts committed outside the territory of the Principality where such acts would constitute crimes under this Code.

Section 5: Sovereignty of the Crown

Nothing in this Code shall limit the authority of the Princess Regnant.

ARTICLE II - GENERAL CRIMINAL LIABILITY

Section 1: Attempt

A person who intentionally engages in conduct constituting a substantial step toward the commission of a crime shall be guilty of an attempt to commit that crime, even if the crime is not completed.

Section 2: Aiding and Abetting

A person who knowingly assists, encourages, facilitates, or supports another in the commission of a crime shall be criminally liable as a principal offender.

Section 3: Solicitation

A person who intentionally solicits, commands, or encourages another to commit a crime shall be criminally liable, regardless of whether the crime is completed.

Section 4: Joint Conduct

Where two or more persons act together, in the commission of a crime, each may be held fully responsible for the offense.

ARTICLE III - CRIMES AGAINST THE STATE

Section 1: Treason

Treason shall consist of:

  • Attempting to overthrow the Government
  • Attempting to undermine the constitutional order of the Principality
  • Assisting foreign entities against the Principality
  • Engaging in espionage against the Principality
  • Committing serious sabotage of national infrastructure

Section 2: Espionage

Espionage shall consist of knowingly and without lawful authority obtaining or transmitting protected state information to unauthorized entities.

Section 3: Sabotage

Sabotage shall consist of deliberate or grossly negligent destruction or impairment of state institutions, military assets, or essential infrastructure.

Section 4: Impersonation of a Government Official

Knowingly presenting oneself as a government official without lawful authority shall constitute a criminal offense.

Section 5: Conspiracy Against the State

Conspiring with others to commit treason, espionage, or sabotage shall constitute a criminal offense.

ARTICLE IV - CRIMES AGAINST PERSONS

Section 1: Murder

Murder shall consist of intentionally causing the death of another person.

Section 2: Manslaughter

Manslaughter shall consist of homicide committed through gross negligence or without premeditated intent.

Section 3: Assault

Intentionally or negligently causing physical harm to another person.

Section 4: Threats and Incitement

Direct threats of violence or incitement to imminent violent action shall constitute a criminal offense.

Speech shall not be criminalized unless it directly incites or threatens violence.

Section 5: Harassment

Persistent conduct intended to intimidate or distress another person unlawfully.

ARTICLE V - CRIMES AGAINST PROPERTY

Section 1: Theft

The unlawful taking of property belonging to another.

Section 2: Fraud

Deception intended to obtain financial or material gain unlawfully.

Section 3: Destruction of Property

Intentional or negligent damage to the property of another.

ARTICLE VI - CRIMES AGAINST PUBLIC ORDER

Section 1: Obstruction of a Government Function

Interfering with lawful government operations or officials in the execution of their duties.

Section 2: Disorderly Conduct

Conduct that significantly disrupts public order or safety.

Section 3: Abuse of Office

Any Civil Servant or official who knowingly uses their position for unlawful gain or harm.

ARTICLE VII - SENTENCING AND PENALTIES

Section 1: Sentencing Authority

Judges shall determine sentences within defined ranges established by this Code.

Section 2: Permissible Penalties

Penalties may include:

  • Fines
  • Imprisonment
  • Removal from office
  • Community Service
  • Suspension of specified civil or political rights as defined by court order
  • Revocation of citizenship
  • Execution

Section 3: Crown Approval

Any sentence involving:

  • Revocation of citizenship
  • Execution

shall require approval by the Princess Regnant before execution.

Section 4: Sentencing Philosophy

Sentences shall reflect a balanced approach considering:

  • Protection of the State
  • Protection of its citizens
  • Rehabilitation of offenders
  • Severity of harm

Section 5: Classification of Offenses

Criminal offenses under this Code shall be classified as follows:

  • Class I - Minor Offenses
  • Class II - Serious Offenses
  • Class III - Grave Offenses
  • Class IV - Capital Offenses

Classification

Section 6: Sentencing Limits

Unless otherwise specified by law:

  • Class I offenses shall not exceed one year imprisonment or fine
  • Class II offenses shall not exceed five years imprisonment
  • Class III offenses shall not exceed twenty-five years imprisonment
  • Class IV offenses may include life imprisonment or capital punishment, subject to approval by the Princess Regnant.

Section 7: Initial Classification

Class I offenses shall include:

  • Harassment
  • Disorderly Conduct

Class II offenses shall include:

  • Assault
  • Theft
  • Fraud
  • Destruction of Property
  • Obstruction of a Government Function
  • Impersonation of a Government Official
  • Threats and Incitement

Class III offenses shall include:

  • Manslaughter
  • Abuse of Office
  • Espionage
  • Sabotage
  • Conspiracy Against the State

Class IV offenses shall include:

  • Murder
  • Treason

These classifications shall stand unless overwritten by subsequent Act.

ARTICLE VIII - SPECIAL PROVISIONS FOR UNIFORMED PERSONNEL

Members of the Armed Forces and Law Enforcement may be subject to additional criminal standards and disciplinary measures under subsequent Acts.

ARTICLE IX - APPEALS

All criminal convictions may be appealed to the appropriate judicial authority under law.

The Princess Regnant retains the authority to grant pardon or clemency.

ARTICLE X - EFFECTIVE DATE

This act shall take effect immediately upon its lawful passage and publication.
