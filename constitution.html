PREAMBLE

We, the citizens of the Principality of Astoria, in recognition of our sovereign Princess Regnant, our shared national identity, and our enduring future, do establish this Constitution to define the structure, function, and continuity of governance of Astoria.

For All Time, For All People, Always

ARTICLE I - THE STATE

Section 1: Name

The state shall be known as The Principality of Astoria.

Section 2: Sovereignty

Sovereignty resides fully and indivisibly in the person of the Princess Regnant, who is the supreme ruler of Astoria. The authority of the Constitution exists by tradition, continuity, and national stability, and does not limit the absolute sovereignty of the Princess Regnant.

Section 3: Language

The Principality shall have no official language

ARTICLE II - THE CROWN

Section 1: Authority

The Princess Regnant is:

  • Head of Government
  • Head of State
  • Supreme Executive Authority
  • Commander-in-Chief of the Royal Astorian Armed Forces

She possesses absolute authority and may:

  • Appoint any person to any office she deems fit
  • Remove any person from any office she deems unfit
  • Propose laws to the Grand Assembly
  • Deny passage any law proposed by the Grand Assembly
  • Issue royal decrees separate of the Grand Assembly
  • Override the Grand Assembly's standard procedure as she deems fit

Her adherence to constitutional tradition is voluntary and customary, not compulsory.

Section 2: Abdication

The Princess Regnant may abdicate her throne through a formal renunciation of the Crown submitted to the Grand Assembly and decreed to the people.

Section 3: Removal

The Princess Regnant may not be removed from office in any way.

Section 4: Royal Decrees

Any Royal Decree issued by the Princess Regnant shall carry the full force of law upon issuance.

Such decrees shall remain in force unless revoked by the Princess Regnant or superseded by subsequent law or decree.

ARTICLE III - SUCCESSION

Section 1: Order of Succession

Succession shall occur under the following order:

  • An heir to the throne as designated by the Princess Regnant.
  • An heir eligible under traditional hereditary succession.
  • An heir selected by the Grand Assembly under an emergency session.

The Princess Regnant shall maintain a publicly designated heir whenever possible.

Section 2: Legislative Emergency Succession

If succession cannot be determined by designation or tradition:

  • The Grand Assembly shall convene an emergency session as outlined in their standard procedures.
  • The session shall not adjourn until a successor to the throne of Princess Regnant is selected.
  • Selection shall follow guidelines as directed by the previous Princess Regnant

Section 3: Citizen Removal Period

For six months following legislative selection, citizens of Astoria may call for a vote to remove the selected Princess Regnant. This removal requires an 80% approval of all votes cast.

If removed, the Grand Assembly must select a new successor under the procedures outlined in A.III S.2.

ARTICLE IV - THE GRAND ASSEMBLY OF ASTORIA

Section 1: Establishment

The legislative body of the Principality of Astoria shall be known as The Grand Assembly of Astoria, hereafter referred to as the Grand Assembly.

The Grand Assembly exists to conduct the ordinary legislative and administrative governance of the Principality under the sovereignty of the Princess Regnant.

Section 2: Composition

The Grand Assembly shall consist of eighteen Members. Members shall be formally known as Members of the Grand Assembly.

Section 3: Terms of Office

Each Member of the Grand Assembly shall serve a term of eight years.

No member may serve more than four total terms in their lifetime. Terms need not be consecutive.

Section 4: Elections and Vacancies

Members may be elected during designated election periods. Vacancies may be filled by:

  • Election
  • Appointment by the Princess Regnant at her discretion

Appointed Members shall possess the same authority and duty as elected Members.

To ensure uninterrupted governance of the Principality, the Princess Regnant may appoint acting officials to any executive office at her discretion.

Such acting officials shall exercise the full authority of the office until replaced, confirmed, or removed by the Princess Regnant.

No vacancy in any office shall impair the sovereign authority of the Crown.

Section 5: Authority

The Grand Assembly shall have the authority to:

  • Draft and pass law
  • Pass and adjust budget and taxation measures
  • Propose constitutional amendments
  • Administer routine governance
  • Temporarily fill offices as required

All legislative authority exists under the sovereignty of the Princess Regnant

Section 6: legislative Voting Thresholds

The following vote thresholds shall apply:

  • Law not covered in the following: Three-fifths majority
  • Budget passage and adjustments, taxation measures: Two-thirds majority
  • Removal of royal appointees: Three-fourths majority
  • Constitutional amendments: Three-fourths majority

Section 7: Removal of Royal Appointees

The Grand Assembly may remove any officeholder appointed by the Princess Regnant by a three-fourths majority vote.

Removal shall take effect immediately, and the removed official shall have the right to appeal.

Temporary replacements may be installed by the Grand Assembly during the appeal process.

Section 8: Legislative Proposals

Legislation may be proposed by:

  • Any Member of the Grand Assembly
  • The Princess Regnant
  • Citizens by petition

Citizen-proposed legislation may not be ignored by the Grand Assembly

Section 9: Internal Officers

The Grand Assembly shall select from among its Members:

  • A President of the Grand Assembly
  • A Deputy President

The President shall preside over legislative sessions and administer legislative procedure.

These positions do not supersede the authority of the Princess Regnant.

Section 10: Relationship to the Crown

The Grand Assembly serves as the primary legislative instrument of the Principality. Its authority exists by constitutional tradition and operates under the sovereignty of the Princess Regnant.

The Princess Regnant retains the authority to:

  • Approve the passage of laws
  • Deny the passage of laws
  • Override standard legislature
  • Issue royal decrees independent of the Grand Assembly

ARTICLE V - CITIZEN POLLING

Section 1: Citizen Polling

Citizens shall be polled monthly to gauge satisfaction with Astorian affairs.

Section 2: Anti-Elections

An official's Anti-Election shall occur if requested by fifteen percent of citizens polled.

Should this Anti-Election obtain a two-thirds majority vote, that official shall be removed from office.

Anti-Elections shall only apply to officials appointed by the Princess Regnant.

The office of the Chief Minister shall not be subject to Anti-Elections and shall remain removable only by the Princess Regnant.

Section 3: Office of the Electorate

Elections and polling shall be conducted by an independent Office of the Electorate, which shall remain permanently independent of the Princess Regnant.

The Office of the Electorate shall be headed by a Director of the Electorate, who shall be appointed by a three-fourths vote of the Grand Assembly and shall serve a term of ten years.

The Director may only be removed by a three-fourths vote by the Grand Assembly.

ARTICLE VI - THE EXECUTIVE GOVERNMENT

Section 1: Executive Authority

Executive authority derives from the Princess Regnant.

Section 2: The Chief Minister

There shall be an office designated the Chief Minister of Astoria.

The Chief Minister:

  • Is appointed by the Princess Regnant
  • Serves without fixed term
  • May be removed as the Princess Regnant sees fit

The Chief Minister shall:

  • Oversee Astorian governmental function
  • Coordinate between ministries
  • Recommend ministers to the Princess Regnant
  • Aid the Princess Regnant in implementing Astorian policy

Section 3: Ministers

There shall be offices of the following designations:

  • Minister of Defense
  • Minister of the Treasury
  • Minister of the Interior
  • Minister of Foreign Affairs
  • Minister of Justice
  • Minister of Communication

They shall:

  • Be recommended by the Chief Minister
  • Be appointed by the Princess Regnant
  • Serve without fixed term

ARTICLE VII - THE JUDICIARY

Section 1: The High Court

The highest court of the Principality shall be named the High Court of Astoria.

This court shall consist of three judges.

Section 2: Appointment

Judges may be appointed by the Princess Regnant or by the Grand Assembly.

A judge shall serve an unlimited term unless removed or resigned.

Section 3: Removal

Judges shall be removed by:

  • The Princess Regnant, or
  • A three-fourth's vote of the Grand Assembly

Section 4: Authority

It is the duty and authority of the High Court to interpret the Constitution and the laws of the Principality.

The High Court may declare legislative acts invalid if they conflict with the Constitution, where the Grand Assembly shall correct or amend such legislation.

The Princess Regnant retains the authority to override any court ruling by formal decree.

Section 5: Rights of the Accused

All persons shall enjoy the right to:

  • Presumption of innocence
  • Fair Trial
  • Jury trial
  • Legal defense

ARTICLE VIII - RIGHTS OF CITIZENS

Citizens shall enjoy the rights to:

  • Freedom of speech
  • Freedom of press
  • Freedom of religion
  • Freedom of assembly
  • Right to privacy
  • Right to property
  • Right to equality
  • Right to due process under law
  • Right to self-defense

ARTICLE IX - DUTIES OF CITIZENS

Citizens shall:

  • Pay taxes to the Ministry of the Treasury
  • Serve as members of the jury
  • Serve the Royal Astorian Armed Forces under drafting order by the Princess Regnant

ARTICLE X - ARMED FORCES

Section 1: Establishment

The military shall be known as the Royal Astorian Armed Forces.

The Princess Regnant shall be known as the Commander-in-Chief.

Section 2: War Powers

A state of war shall only be declared by the Princess Regnant.

Section 3: The Crown Guard

There shall exist a unit of the military at all designated as the Crown Guard, which shall serve as the personal guard of the Princess Regnant and protector of the Crown.

The Crown Guard shall answer solely to the Princess Regnant, and shall exist independently of the standard military chain of command except where directed by the Princess Regnant.

Section 4: Oath of Military Loyalty

All members of the Royal Astorian Armed Forces and the Crown Guard shall swear loyalty to:

  • The Principality of Astoria
  • The Constitution
  • The Princess Regnant

Their loyalty to the Princess Regnant shall be paramount within the chain of command.

ARTICLE XI - CONSTITUTIONAL AMENDMENTS

Amendments to the Constitution shall be proposed by the Princess Regnant or the Grand Assembly.

The final draft shall be voted for by the citizens, where a three-fourth's vote shall be obtained to pass.

ARTICLE XII - PERMANENCE OF THE MONARCHY

The Monarchy shall be permanent and may not be abolished by any citizen or member of government

ARTICLE XIII - CITIZENSHIP

Section 1: Obtaining

Any person may obtain citizenship to the Principality of Astoria through birth, or through legal application through the Ministry of the Interior.

Section 2: Voting

All citizens over the age of eighteen shall enjoy the right to vote.

ARTICLE XIV - POLITICAL SYSTEM

The Principality of Astoria shall remain nonpartisan in all affairs.

No political party may be recognized by the Office of the Electorate or the Grand Assembly.

ARTICLE XV - OATH OF OFFICE

All officials of the Principality of Astoria, whether appointed by the Princess Regnant or elected by the citizens, shall swear loyalty to:

  • Astoria
  • The Constitution
  • The Princess Regnant
